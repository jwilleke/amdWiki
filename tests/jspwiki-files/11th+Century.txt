!! Overview
[{$pagename}] is the [Century] from [1001|Year 1001] (represented by the Roman numerals MI) through [1100|Year 1100] (MC) in accordance with the [Julian calendar|Julian Calendar], and the first century of the [2nd Millennium].

In [China|China], the [Song dynasty] continued to flourish, advancing in technology, economy, and culture, with innovations like movable type and gunpowder emerging. The [Muslim] World saw the decline of the [Caliphate of CÃ³rdoba] in al-Andalus, fragmentation of the [Abbasid Caliphate], and the rise of the [Fatimid Caliphate] in [Northern Africa] and the [Middle East]. The [Byzantine Empire] faced challenges but maintained cultural and intellectual vitality, while the [Holy Roman Empire] saw the continuation of the Ottonian dynasty and the rise of the [Salian dynasty]. In [Europe], the period marked the beginning of the High [Middle Ages], with [feudalism] solidifying and the spread of [Christianity], notably through events like the Christianization of [Kievan Rus'|Kievan Rus']. The [{$pagename}] also saw the [Norman Conquest] of [England] in 1066 and the start of the [Crusades] with the First Crusade in 1096-1099. [{$pagename}] is often seen as a transition from the Dark Ages to a period of cultural and economic growth, though regional conflicts and instability persisted.
